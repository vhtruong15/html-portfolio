The Best Movies According to Vy

My top 3 animated films of all-times

A Silent Voice (2016)

A Japanese animated drama film based on the manga by Yoshitoki Oima and produced by Kyoto Animation.
The film covers elements of coming of age through psychological drama with the theme of bullying, disability and forgiveness. The story begins with Shoya Ishida and Shouko Nishimiya in elementary school forcing one of their students to transfer out to another school due to their hearing impairment. Years later, the students come back together, with Shoya setting himself on a path to redemption.

A plot with a beautiful representation of hardship among peers.

Kubo and the Two Strings (2016)

An American animated action fantasy film produced by studio Laika.
The movie centered on a child named Kubo where he accidentally summons a vengeful spirit from the past into his world. Now on the run, Kubo arms himself with a magical instrument and joins forces with Monkey and Beetle to unlock a secret legacy. Kubo finds himself fighting the Moon King and other gods and monsters to save his family and solve the mystery of his fallen father, the greatest samurai warrior the world has ever known.

Admiration of a simple story told through the power of stop-motion.

Belle (2021)

A Japanese animated science fantasy film written and directed by Mamoru Hosoda.
The movie revolves around a high school student who becomes a globally beloved singer after entering a fantastic virtual world. She soon embarks on an emotional and epic quest to uncover the identity of a mysterious beast who's on the run from ruthless vigilantes.

Stunning visuals with strong, emotional music compositions throughout the film.
